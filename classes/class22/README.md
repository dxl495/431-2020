## Project B

As I hope you know by now, Project B involves two studies.

In Study 1, each project team will work with the same data, from the Class Survey of students in 2019 and 2020, and I [provided those data in full to you](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/survey3.html), late last night, and adjusted the Study 1 instructions on getting the data accordingly.

- You now have everything you need to accomplish all of Study 1, including a Demonstration project.

In Study 2, you are [responsible for selecting your data source](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your1.html), and everyone as a result could be working with different data. You have all of the information I plan to give you to let you obtain data for Study 2. Fundamentally, I have suggested two options for your data for this Study.

1. Option 1 is to use data from the [National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey, or NHANES](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your2.html). 
2. Option 2 is to use any other source [that meets with my specifications](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your3.html).

Within the next week (by 2020-11-19) you will put together [your Study 2 Data Plan](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your5.html), where you specify the data you will use, and answer a series of questions about it. I am agnostic about whether you choose option 1 or option 2, and do not favor either.

- If you have an available data set that you want to study that [meets my specifications](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your3.html), please feel encouraged to use it.
- If you don't have a data set in mind, or if you're just interested in studying the things that NHANES measures, including health, nutrition, disparities, social influences of health, and many related issues, then I encourage you to [use data from NHANES](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your2.html).

The only thing still to come for Study 2 (and this will appear before the Thanksgiving Break) is a [Study 2 Demonstration Project](https://thomaselove.github.io/431-2020-projectB/your7.html). 
- Note that the Demonstration Project uses simulated data, so while it should be very helpful to you for thinking about completing analyses using R, it won't be of any help to you at all in selecting your Study 2 data set.
